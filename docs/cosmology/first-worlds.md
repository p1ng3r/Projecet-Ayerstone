# The First Worlds

## Before the Modern Void

The **First Worlds** are the oldest civilizations remembered in broken myth, ruin fragments, void songs, and forbidden comparative histories.

They existed in an age when mortals, Pillar Titans, Veiled Sovereigns, and raw magic may have touched the same reality.

No complete record survives.

What remains is enough to trouble anyone who reads too deeply.

## Magic as Pressure

The First World empires discovered that magic was not merely energy.

It was pressure from beyond the ordered world.

Every great working pulled on the fabric of existence. Every spell was a small negotiation. Every ritual was a lever placed against reality.

At first, this knowledge made wonders.

Then it made weapons.

Then it made engines.

## Titans as Anchors

Some First World cultures treated Pillar Titans as sacred world-shapers. Others learned to use them as anchors for vast workings.

A Stone Titan could stabilize a continent.

A Water Titan could regulate oceans.

A Storm Titan could power sky-engines.

A Gravity Titan could bend the weight of cities.

This did not mean the Titans consented.

## Sovereigns as Patrons

The Veiled Sovereigns were invoked differently.

They were not chained to mountains or engines. They were called as patrons, muses, witnesses, advisors, weapons, and judges.

A Sovereign of Memory could preserve a civilization's records beyond death.

A Sovereign of Love could bind families across worlds.

A Sovereign of Hunger could make an army never surrender.

Every bargain had a price.

Many First World cultures paid it without understanding the currency.

## The Mistake

Every ancient tradition eventually reaches the same shadow.

Something beyond the known Void noticed.

Some call it:

- The Star-Eater Host
- The Hunger Outside Flame
- The Night Beyond Names
- The Mouth Between Worlds
- The Dark That Learned the Door

It came not as an army, but as appetite.

Stars dimmed.

Worlds hollowed.

Pillar Titans went mad.

Veiled Sovereigns withdrew, lied, slept, or turned against their worshippers.

Civilizations built larger and larger engines to flee, fight, hide, or seal themselves away.

## The Shattering of the First Worlds

No single account explains the fall.

Some scholars blame the Star-Eater Host.

Others blame the Titan War.

Some blame the First World empires themselves, arguing that their engines, prisons, rituals, and Titan bindings broke the laws they depended upon.

The likely truth is worse:

All of these may have happened at once.

The First Worlds shattered in stages. Some were consumed. Some fled. Some broke. Some vanished. Some may have folded themselves into unreachable spaces. Some may still exist, but no longer in forms mortals would call worlds.

## Legacy

Modern Arkflight may be a distant echo of First World technology.

Scholars have found ancient ruins containing:

- Containment rings
- Pressure lattices
- Core cradles
- Resonance channels
- Life-envelope inscriptions
- Unknown glyphs older than current languages
- Planet-scale engineering marks

The earliest Arkengines may not have been invented from nothing.

They may have been rediscovered.

Most captains do not like hearing this.

## Public Understanding

Most citizens do not discuss the First Worlds in daily life. To them, First World stories are scholar talk, temple mystery, or sailor superstition.

Yet the ruins are real.

The relics are real.

The old glyphs are real.

And the Void still contains things that look disturbingly like the wreckage of civilizations that tried to master forces now barely understood.

## Common Saying

> The First Worlds did not fall in darkness. They lit too many fires.

Other sayings:

> Every old ruin is a warning pretending to be treasure.

> The past is not dead in the Void. It is drifting.
