# Shadow Stars

## Stars That Emit Absence

Some stars do not emit light.

They emit absence.

These are known as **Shadow Stars**, though the name is more poetic than scientific. Navigators mark them with black circles. Priests argue over whether they are dead suns, wounds in reality, devoured stars, or eyes looking inward from somewhere no ship should go.

Whatever they are, ships avoid them when possible.

## Appearance

A Shadow Star is not simply a dark point in the sky. It is a region where the surrounding stars seem dimmer, where route instruments hesitate, and where crews often feel that something important has been forgotten.

Seen through certain lenses, a Shadow Star may appear as:

- A black sun with no corona
- A hole in the starfield
- A negative flame
- A star-shaped absence
- A circular bruise in the Void
- A lightless eye

## Known Effects

Shadow Stars are associated with:

- Navigation errors
- Missing memories
- Vanished ships
- Failed route readings
- Crew disorientation
- Compass disagreement
- False distress signals
- Dreams of falling upward
- Lifeveil pressure changes
- Arkengine hesitation

A few ships return after passing too close.

Some return wrong.

## Theological Interpretations

Different faiths disagree sharply about Shadow Stars.

Aelorian priests often call them **Unlit Horizons** and teach that travelers should not mistake every darkness for a road.

Churn Readers sometimes claim Shadow Stars are places where lives were interrupted before their purpose could complete.

Veilwardens treat them as containment risks.

The Brotherhood of the Cosmic Flame considers them insults to cosmic fire.

Freespacers generally consider them bad news and change course.

## The Crownless Sun

One infamous Shadow Star is known as **the Crownless Sun**.

It has no corona, no warmth, and no reliable position on certain charts. Several doomed cults have worshipped it, claiming it is not dead but waiting.

Captains who sail near the Crownless Sun often report identical dreams: a throne with no ruler, a crown with no head, and a door opening into a lightless room.

## Sailor Rules

Common rules include:

- Never count crew while facing a Shadow Star.
- Never sleep on open deck beneath one.
- Never use a Shadow Star as a navigation point.
- Never answer a voice heard during Shadow Star passage.
- Never trust a memory that begins with black light.

## Adventure Hooks

- A ship returns from a Shadow Star route with every logbook page blank.
- A navigator insists a Shadow Star has moved closer to Ayerstone.
- A cult in Driftwall claims the Crownless Sun is visible from a sealed cellar.
- A Lifeveil begins showing a dark star that is not in the sky.
- A Starweaver chart includes a safe route around a Shadow Star, but the route was never officially mapped.

## Common Saying

> Some stars guide. Some stars erase.

Other sayings:

> Never steer by a light that refuses to shine.

> The dark between stars is honest. The dark shaped like a star is not.
