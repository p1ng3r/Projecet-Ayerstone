# The Veiled Sovereigns

## Powers Behind Thought

Alongside the Pillar Titans existed stranger beings, older or perhaps simply less physical.

Modern scholars call them the **Veiled Sovereigns**.

Where the Pillar Titans embodied matter and force, the Veiled Sovereigns embodied the invisible powers that shape mortal life: memory, hunger, grief, dreams, love, fear, oaths, endings, balance, and becoming.

They did not stride across worlds like mountains.

They moved through thought, destiny, culture, and soul.

A Pillar Titan could break a continent.

A Veiled Sovereign could make a civilization forget why it was founded.

## Why They Are Feared

The ominous thing about the Veiled Sovereigns is that some scholars believe they never died, never left, and were never truly defeated.

They simply became harder to perceive.

A god may demand worship.

A Sovereign may not care whether anyone remembers it.

That does not mean it is gone.

## Known Domains

Ancient fragments associate the Veiled Sovereigns with abstractions such as:

- Mind
- Spirit
- Love
- Hunger
- Memory
- Grief
- Fear
- Oaths
- Dreams
- Balance
- Endings
- Becoming

Some traditions claim there were originally thirteen Sovereigns.

Only seven are remembered clearly.

The rest are missing from history.

This terrifies historians, because the obvious question remains:

> How do you forget a god?

## The Thirteen Lost Sovereigns

The following Sovereigns appear in recurring records across multiple cultures. Their names differ, but their symbols and domains are disturbingly consistent.

### The Sovereign of Memory

Known names include:

- The Remembering King
- The Archivist
- The One Who Counts

Symbol: a crown made of keys.

Domain: memory, history, record, legacy, and the burden of knowing.

Scholars fear that if this Sovereign dies, history itself may change.

### The Sovereign of Love

Known names include:

- The Heart Between Stars
- The First Companion
- The Vow-Warmth

Domain: love, loyalty, family, devotion, and bonds that survive distance.

Its followers claim:

> No voyage is truly made alone.

### The Sovereign of Hunger

Known names include:

- The Mouth Beyond
- The Empty King
- The Wanting Dark

Domain: consumption, need, desire, appetite, longing, and endless taking.

Many believe the Star-Eater myths descend from this Sovereign or from powers that served it.

### The Sovereign of Dreams

Known names include:

- The Sleepless Sailor
- The Dreaming Gate
- The Unclosed Eye

Domain: dreams, possibility, imagination, prophecy, and unreal journeys.

Readers of the Churn frequently claim to encounter this being in symbolic forms.

### The Sovereign of Balance

Known names include:

- The Still Scale
- The Equal Dark
- The Measure

Domain: balance, equilibrium, correction, consequence, and cosmic accounting.

It is not considered kind.

It is considered necessary.

### The Sovereign of Endings

Known names include:

- The Last Door
- The Quiet Hand
- The Closing Star

Domain: endings, death, completion, closure, and release.

Unlike death gods of later religions, this Sovereign is rarely depicted as cruel. It simply finishes what has reached its end.

### The Sovereign of Becoming

Known names include:

- The Unfinished Crown
- The Molting One
- The Road That Changes

Domain: transformation, identity, growth, mutation, choice, and the terror of becoming something new.

Some artists and revolutionaries secretly revere this Sovereign.

## The Forgotten Six

Ancient records suggest there were thirteen Sovereigns.

Six are missing.

Their names are scraped away, overwritten, burned, blank, or replaced by empty spaces.

In some ruins, thirteen symbols appear in a ring, but six have been carved out so completely that even the surrounding stone seems strained.

This absence is one of the most disturbing mysteries in comparative theology.

## Worship and Study

Most modern churches do not openly worship the Veiled Sovereigns.

Some call them proto-gods.

Some call them demons.

Some call them metaphysical principles.

Some refuse to speak of them at all.

Occult scholars, void mystics, forbidden archivists, Churn Readers, and certain secret societies study them anyway.

## Sovereigns in Daily Life

Ordinary people may invoke Sovereign fragments without realizing it.

A grandmother saying, "memory keeps the dead warm" may be repeating a phrase once tied to the Remembering King.

A sailor saying, "no voyage is made alone" may be echoing the Sovereign of Love.

A gambler whispering, "the Scale always settles" may be invoking the Still Scale.

Old powers survive in small words.

## Common Forbidden Saying

> The Titans made the worlds. The Sovereigns taught them how to suffer.

Other sayings:

> Do not pray to what does not need you.

> A forgotten name is still a name.

> Some gods die when forgotten. Some are freed by it.
