# Skullwatch Depot

## Frontier Village of Vael'Tharis

Skullwatch Depot is the only stable settlement on Vael'Tharis. It is less a finished village than a fortified foothold struggling to become one.

Roughly 700 permanent residents live here, though the number rises and falls with adventurers, prospectors, guards, scholars, traders, and desperate fortune seekers.

## Recent History

Vael'Tharis was connected to Ayerstone only within the last five to six years. The first two attempts to establish a settlement were overrun by strange lizard creatures, kobolds, lizardfolk, and yuan-ti attacks.

The current Skullwatch has survived for roughly one and a half to two years, making it the most successful outpost on the island so far.

## Layout

The village contains around 30 buildings inside a defensive wall.

Most structures are rough timber, stone, salvaged ship material, canvas, and reinforced palisade work. The settlement is small, tense, muddy, and crowded with people who know they are very far from safe.

## Major Buildings

### The Library

One of the largest stone structures in the village. It is being built by a group viewed either as dragon cultists or religious fanatics, depending on who is speaking.

They claim they are preserving the memory of Aurelion Vaelthar.

### Wallguard Barracks

The other major stone structure. It houses the guards responsible for watching the wall and responding to attacks from the jungle.

### The Two Inns

Skullwatch has two combined taverns and inns, both serving prospectors, guards, scholars, and adventurers preparing to enter the island.

## Economy

Most residents are prospectors, suppliers, guards, guides, herbalists, or workers tied to Celestial Trade interests.

Adventurers bring money, stories, trouble, and bodies.

## Travel

The Celestial Trade and Shipping Consortium maintains a depot here. Flying horses owned by the company ferry people to and from the island more safely than Arkengine vessels.

Arkengine ships avoid close approach because of the shimmering veil and the strange reactions it causes in their engines.

## Local Saying

> Many enter the island. Few come back. Fewer come back unchanged.
