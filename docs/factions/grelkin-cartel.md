# The Grelkin Cartel

## Raiders, Pirates, and Stolen Ships

The **Grelkin Cartel** is a harsh raider empire made up largely of orcs, goblins, hobgoblins, and kobolds.

Outsiders call it a cartel because it is built on piracy, resource theft, tribute, war profit, and clan-controlled trade. Its own people often call it simply **the Cartel** or **the Grelkin Holdfasts**.

The Grelkin do not pretend to be noble liberators or enlightened rulers.

They take what they need.

They keep what they can defend.

## Founding Peoples

The Cartel is dominated by four peoples:

- Orcs
- Goblins
- Hobgoblins
- Kobolds

Other peoples may live within Cartel space, but these four hold most of the military, political, and economic power.

Orcs often dominate war leadership and boarding parties.

Hobgoblins provide discipline, command structure, logistics, and fortress rule.

Goblins drive salvage, invention, repair, and black-market trade.

Kobolds serve as engineers, tunnel crews, trapwrights, scouts, and engine scavengers.

## Government

The Grelkin Cartel rules its empire strictly.

Its territories are divided between clans, war chiefs, pirate lords, fortress bosses, ship-kings, and resource commanders. Authority is maintained through strength, wealth, fear, tribute, and the ability to deliver prizes.

A weak ruler loses ships.

A ruler who loses ships loses loyalty.

A ruler who loses loyalty does not remain ruler long.

## Economy

The Cartel is best known for raiding, piracy, and resource seizure.

Its ships leave Cartel territory to strike convoys, frontier settlements, mining stations, isolated trade ships, and poorly defended outposts. They take ore, fuel, food, prisoners, weapons, ship parts, cargo, maps, and anything else useful.

The Cartel also engages in salvage, black-market trade, mercenary work, and protection rackets.

To the Grelkin, ownership is proven by defense.

If you could not protect it, you did not deserve it.

## Ships

Grelkin ships are often archaic, brutal, patched, and stolen.

Many begin life as vessels from other major factions: Brotherhood warships, Freespacer cutters, Starweaver merchant craft, Veilwarden escorts, or independent haulers.

Once captured, they are repaired, reinforced, stripped, rebuilt, rearmed, repainted, renamed, and made ugly in a distinctly Grelkin way.

Common features include:

- Black or rust-red sails
- Patchwork hull plates
- Reinforced boarding prows
- Heavy grappling hooks
- Scrap armor
- Spiked railings
- Trophy chains
- Mismatched cannons
- Exposed repairs
- Stolen figureheads
- Loud engines that somehow still work

Every Grelkin ship tells the story of someone else's defeat.

## Fleet Doctrine

The Cartel favors:

- Ambushes
- Boarding actions
- Convoy raids
- Fast strikes
- Feigned retreats
- Resource theft
- Salvage after battle
- Hit-and-run piracy

Grelkin captains rarely seek clean conquest when theft will do.

They prefer targets that are valuable, vulnerable, and far from help.

## Culture

The Grelkin are not mindless marauders.

They value strength, loyalty, cleverness, clan obligation, practical skill, and the ability to survive terrible odds.

Stealing from outsiders may be celebrated.

Stealing from your own clan is one of the gravest crimes imaginable.

Many Grelkin crews are brutally loyal to one another. A captain who feeds their crew, wins prizes, and gets them home can inspire fierce devotion.

## Relationship with Ayerstone

Ayerstone's Open Dock Compact allows Grelkin vessels to dock, provided they obey harbor law.

This makes the Grand Docks complicated.

A Grelkin ship may be a pirate vessel, a trade partner, a mercenary contractor, a salvage crew, a wanted raider, or all of those things depending on who is speaking.

The Irystone Watch keeps a close eye on Grelkin crews.

The Underwake often does business with them.

Many Freespacers distrust them but respect their independence.

## Public Reputation

In Ayerstone, Grelkin are associated with:

- Piracy
- Resource raids
- Stolen ships
- Harsh clan law
- Brutal boarding actions
- Skilled salvage
- Unpredictable deals
- Fierce loyalty within crews

A dockworker might curse them in the morning and sell them rope by evening.

## Symbol

A broken chain wrapped around a jagged boarding axe over a black sail.

## Adventure Hooks

- A Grelkin captain offers to sell back a ship they stole from a noble house.
- A captured Brotherhood vessel arrives flying Grelkin colors and carrying something worse than cargo.
- A Cartel clan hires adventurers to recover a stolen engine part from a rival clan.
- Grelkin raiders claim they attacked only because their home station is starving.
- A goblin salvage boss has a map to a Grave Reef full of ancient wreckage.
- A hobgoblin commander demands trial by harbor law after being accused of piracy.
- A kobold engineer offers to fix a ship faster than the Guild of Arkengineers and for half the price.

## Common Sayings

> Take what you need. Keep what you can.

> A good ship is a captured ship.

> If they still own it, they were not using it hard enough.

> Steel is cheaper than diplomacy.
