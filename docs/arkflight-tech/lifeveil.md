# Lifeveil

## The Breath Around the Ship

The **Lifeveil** is the breathable atmospheric envelope surrounding a void-capable Arkflight ship. Among older sailors and rougher crews it is sometimes called the **Airskin**.

Without a Lifeveil, Arkflight travel would be impossible for ordinary crews. The Void is not a sea of air. It is hostile, cold, pressureless, magically unstable, and full of regions where ordinary life cannot survive.

The Lifeveil is the thin miracle that lets people stand on deck beneath the stars and live.

## What the Lifeveil Does

A functioning Lifeveil helps a ship:

- Hold breathable air
- Regulate pressure
- Moderate temperature
- Resist void exposure
- Protect open decks
- Stabilize living quarters
- Support long voyages
- Buffer minor magical turbulence
- Keep passengers from understanding how close death really is

Most passengers never think about the Lifeveil.

Every sailor does.

## Appearance

A healthy Lifeveil is usually invisible or nearly invisible. In certain light, it may shimmer at the edges of a ship like heat haze, soap film, or moonlit glass.

During stress, it may become visible as:

- Blue-white ripples
- Silver arcs
- Pale green pressure bands
- Frost patterns in the air
- Small floating motes
- A faint aurora around the hull

When a Lifeveil flickers, people stop laughing.

## How It Works

The Lifeveil is sustained by a combination of Arkengine output, aetherite resonance, shipboard latticework, purification systems, and atmospheric renewal.

It is not simply a bubble. It is a regulated living envelope of air, pressure, warmth, and magical stability.

The system is maintained through:

- Lifeveil lattice nodes
- Aetherite-fed regulators
- Air renewal charms
- Pressure seals
- Hull runes
- Arkengine resonance
- Purification vents
- Druidic or alchemical air systems

## The Lifeveil Lattice

The **Lifeveil Lattice** is the network of runes, conduits, vents, crystal nodes, and regulators that shapes the breathable field around the ship.

It runs through the hull like a second nervous system.

Damaged lattice nodes can cause dead air pockets, pressure flutters, localized cold zones, strange smells, or patches where sound behaves incorrectly.

## Connection to the Arkengine

The Arkengine does not always create the Lifeveil entirely by itself, but it helps stabilize and feed the system during travel.

A failing engine may cause the Lifeveil to:

- Thin
- Flicker
- Shrink
- Pulse
- Distort sound
- Grow cold
- Pull inward toward the hull
- Shed sparks or mist

This is why engineers and veilkeepers argue constantly over who caused the problem.

## Veilkeepers

Many ships employ a specialist known as a **Veilkeeper** or **Veilwarden**. Their job is to monitor the Lifeveil, maintain lattice nodes, track air purity, regulate pressure, and coordinate with the engineer during difficult travel.

A good Veilkeeper is calm, observant, and deeply unpleasant to ignore.

Common Veilkeeper tools include:

- Air bells
- Pressure beads
- Breath candles
- Silver-thread gauges
- Lattice tuning rods
- Purity charms
- Emergency veil patches

## Air and Renewal

Lifeveil air is not infinite.

Ships must renew, scrub, and stabilize their breathable envelope during long journeys. This may involve alchemical filters, plant systems, druidic maintenance, water cycling, crystal purification, and periodic port servicing.

Druidic knowledge dramatically improved long-range Lifeveil sustainability. Modern longhaul vessels often include small green rooms, moss tanks, air gardens, or living purification frames.

## Failure Signs

Early warning signs include:

- Candles burning blue
- Breath fog indoors
- Pressure headaches
- Sudden nausea
- Metallic taste
- Deck frost
- Small animals panicking
- Crew hearing their own heartbeat too loudly
- Lantern flames leaning toward the hull
- Air bells ringing without wind

Serious warning signs include:

- Visible cracks in the veil
- Air rushing toward one side of the ship
- Sound vanishing on deck
- Crew gasping near open railings
- Lifeveil glow turning black or red
- Objects drifting outward from the hull

## Emergency Measures

During Lifeveil failure, crews may:

- Reduce speed
- Divert Arkengine output
- Seal lower decks
- Collapse the Lifeveil inward
- Abandon exposed deck stations
- Burn emergency spell reserves
- Activate backup lattice nodes
- Use portable breath charms
- Land if any landing exists

The harshest captain's order is:

> Bring the veil in.

It means the outer decks are being sacrificed so the inner ship can breathe.

## Common Superstitions

Sailors believe:

- Never spit through the Lifeveil edge.
- Never throw bones overboard during thin air.
- Never whistle while the veil is reforming.
- Always thank the Veilkeeper after a storm.
- If the veil glows green, something living is nearby.
- If the veil reflects stars that are not there, wake the navigator.

## Common Saying

> A hull keeps you aboard. A Lifeveil keeps you alive.

Other sayings:

> Air is only cheap in port.

> Trust the sailor who watches the edge of the deck.

> The Void only needs one breath from you.
