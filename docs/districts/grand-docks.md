# The Grand Docks

## Gateway of Ayerstone

*The last light before the dark.*

The Grand Docks dominate the entire southwestern face of Ayerstone's central island. They are not a simple harbor; they are a cliffside city of platforms, cranes, cargo lifts, rope bridges, warehouses, taverns, shrines, customs houses, and Arkflight berths.

From the lowest cheap platforms to the white-marble elite docks above, the Grand Docks rise in stacked tiers up the side of Ayerstone. Stairs, cargo ramps, rudimentary lifts, and levstone elevators connect the levels. Merchants clutter every tier with stalls, crates, awnings, cookfires, repair booths, and temporary contracts.

At full capacity, the Grand Docks can berth roughly 300 Arkflight ships at once.

## Layout

### Lower Docks

The cheapest and roughest berths. Independent captains, adventurers, damaged ships, cheap haulers, and desperate crews end up here.

### Middle Docks

The busiest working levels. Cargo moves constantly between warehouses, cranes, customs offices, ropewrights, chandlers, food stalls, repair crews, and merchant booths.

### Upper Docks

Prestigious white-marble terraces near the top of the southwest cliffs. Famous captains, noble vessels, diplomatic ships, and renowned merchant craft dock here.

## Major Locations

### The Last Lantern

The most famous sailor's tavern in the Grand Docks, tucked near the lower platforms. Its motto is: *The last light before the dark.*

### Celestial Trade and Shipping Consortium

A massive white-marble trade hall on the Upper Docks, controlled by Lady Celestia Starweaver and House Starweaver interests.

### Twin Sentinels of the Horizon

Two colossal statues of Aeloria, Lady of the Endless Horizon, stand over the Upper Docks. One welcomes returning ships. One calls departing ships into the Void.

### Guild of Arkengineers

Officially the Guild of Arkengineers, almost everyone calls them the Arkflight Mechanics. They certify and maintain the specialists who keep Arkengines alive.

## Dockside Saying

> If it sails the Void, it eventually passes through here.
