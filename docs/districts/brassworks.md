# Brassworks

## The Furnace Quarter

Brassworks is Ayerstone's industrial district: a place of foundries, machine halls, smithies, casting yards, furnace towers, coal lifts, brass pipes, tool shops, engine fittings, armorers, and constant hammering.

House Emberhall is the dominant power here, but many smaller workshops, guild foundries, toolmakers, armorers, machinists, bell casters, chainworkers, and machinewrights operate under its shadow.

## Appearance

The district is built from dark brick, black iron, soot-stained stone, brass rails, smokestacks, gantries, heavy foundations, enclosed furnaces, pressure vents, and guarded machine halls.

At night, furnace glow stains the streets orange. Sparks drift upward like fireflies. The air smells of hot metal, oil, coal, wet stone, and forge smoke.

## Layout

Brassworks sits in the lower industrial stretch of Ayerstone, tied by trade roads to the Grand Docks, Deepstone Gate, and Merchant's Reach.

Materials come in from Deepstone Gate and the docks. Finished goods leave for ships, markets, houses, and construction sites across the city.

## Major Features

### Furnace Rows

Long avenues of connected workshops and foundries. Some are family-owned. Others belong to Emberhall contracts, guild cooperatives, or shipbuilding suppliers.

### Casting Yards

Open industrial courts used for large metal pours, chain repair, ship fittings, anchors, industrial components, armor plates, crane parts, and experimental machinery.

### Workers' Kitchens

Large communal food halls serving shift workers at all hours. These are loud, cheap, hot, and essential.

### Emberhall Forgekeep

The public seat of House Emberhall's industrial authority.

It is part office, part foundry, part guild court, part fortress of contracts. Its chimneys burn clean but bright, a deliberate display of wealth and technical control.

### The Chainwright Courts

Specialized workshops capable of repairing, testing, and fitting massive chain components. Some claim that only old Ironmantle records and Emberhall techniques together can truly maintain the great island chains.

### Brasspipe Alleys

Narrow service lanes lined with pipes, valves, gauges, vents, and heat channels. Outsiders get lost. Workers do not.

## Culture

Brassworks respects competence more than title. Nobles who cannot name the tool in their hand are treated with polite contempt.

Apprentices start young. Masters are known by scars, calluses, burns, inventions, and the sounds of their hammers.

The district is proud, blunt, loud, and useful.

## House Emberhall

House Emberhall's influence is strongest here. It controls major foundries, industrial contracts, furnace rights, supply chains, and technical guild relationships.

But Emberhall cannot own every hand that works metal. Independent smiths are fiercely proud of that fact.

- A missing apprentice is found alive inside a sealed machine.
- A prototype levstone crane lifts itself free and drifts over the district.
