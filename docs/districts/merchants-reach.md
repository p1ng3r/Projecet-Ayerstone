# Merchant's Reach

## The Commercial Heart of Ayerstone

*If it exists, you can find it in the Reach.*

Merchant's Reach occupies much of the northeastern quarter of Ayerstone, opposite the Grand Docks. Officially it is called Merchant's Reach, though older locals still call it the Grand Exchange or Exchange Ward.

At the center of the district is the Reach Market, a sprawling field of stalls, booths, tents, kiosks, merchant pavilions, auction platforms, and open plazas. Unlike many parts of Ayerstone, the center is not packed wall-to-wall with buildings. It has room to breathe, bargain, shout, perform, and sell.

Outside the center, merchant buildings, counting houses, guild halls, warehouses, shops, money changers, and taverns spread across the district.

## Famous Locations

### The Tipsy Toad

The most famous tavern in Merchant's Reach. It is owned by Grommick "Tophat" Brindletooth, a half-orc who rose from poverty into wealth through style, charm, and ruthless good sense.

Grommick is known for fine suits, a top hat, and an ornate levstone sword cane he sometimes leaves floating in the air before casually taking it back.

### Meatropolis

A famous butcher and exotic meat market run by Sylvara Mistwind, a lithe sylph widow in her late forties. Meatropolis prides itself on delicacies from the far reaches of the Void and is staffed mostly by halflings.

### Forgeheart and Sons

The most renowned arms shop in the district, run by three dwarven triplet brothers and their descendants. The family claims descent from the smiths who forged the chains connecting the outer islands to true Ayerstone.

### Verdant Crucible Alchemy

A sizeable alchemy shop run by a gnomish rock druid, an elf alchemist, and a male centaur who also serves as a high priest near the waterfront.

## Local Joke

Visitors ask whether anyone lives in Merchant's Reach.

Locals answer: *Officially? No.*

Many shops operate constantly, and some merchants seem to have forgotten where their homes are.
