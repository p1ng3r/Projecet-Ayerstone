# Waterfall Ward

## Canals and Living Water

Waterfall Ward follows the canals, river channels, reservoirs, and civic waterworks fed by Portal Lake. It is one of Ayerstone's most important working districts, though visitors often overlook it in favor of the Grand Docks or Merchant's Reach.

House Tidereach holds immense influence here.

## Appearance

White stone bridges, blue tile, public fountains, water clocks, canal locks, reservoir towers, rain chains, mist gardens, water mills, sluice gates, water shrines, and maintenance platforms define the ward.

Small boats and floating work barges move constantly through the channels. Workers shout measurements. Children race leaf boats. Priests bless fountains. Engineers argue about flow.

## Layout

Waterfall Ward is not one neighborhood. It is a chain of water-linked spaces that follow Ayerstone's controlled waterways from Portal Lake toward lower terraces and edgefalls.

Some streets are dry and formal. Others are damp, narrow, and crossed by little bridges. Some buildings are half built over water.

## Major Features

### Canal Locks

Systems of gates and controlled channels that guide water through the city. These prevent flooding, regulate district supply, and direct flow toward reservoirs, fountains, mills, and edgefalls.

### Public Reservoirs

Large civic water stores where clean water is measured, blessed, guarded, and distributed. Many neighborhoods identify themselves by which reservoir serves them.

### Waterhouses

Combination bathhouses, washhouses, tea houses, public meeting halls, and neighborhood message centers.

Waterhouses are where ordinary citizens argue, gossip, wash clothes, hire workers, share news, and cool off during festival days.

### Tidereach Offices

Elegant but practical administrative houses where engineers, clerks, waterkeepers, and magistrates manage flow charts, water rights, repairs, tariffs, canal priorities, and disputes.

### The Blue Steps

A broad stairway descending beside a controlled waterfall, covered in blue tile and polished by generations of wet footsteps.

### Mist Mills

Water-powered mills and small workshops that use controlled flow from Portal Lake.

## House Tidereach

House Tidereach's power here is everywhere: in the measurements, locks, clerks, water-right seals, canal contracts, and blue-white uniforms of waterkeepers.

They do not rule the district openly, but everyone knows which house can make a fountain dry.

## Culture

Waterfall Ward is quieter than Merchant's Reach but never still. Citizens come here to wash, drink, pray, argue over water rights, repair canals, and watch festival lanterns drift on the channels.

Clean water is civic pride. Wasting water is treated as rude, foolish, and sometimes criminal.

## Dangers

- Sudden pressure changes
- Locked gates opening at the wrong time
- Slippery bridge stones
- Hidden water tunnels
- Things living in old channels
- Sabotage by rival houses
- Flooding in lower lanes

## Adventure Hooks

- A canal lock opens by itself every night at the same hour.
- A waterhouse mirror shows a chamber beneath Portal Lake.
- House Tidereach quietly hires help to inspect a sealed channel.
- Someone is poisoning a reservoir but only on festival nights.
- A child vanishes while racing leaf boats through a forbidden sluice.
- A water clock begins counting down to an unknown event.

## Local Sayings

> Most people only notice water when it is gone.

> Follow the canal and you will find who profits.

> Tidereach does not raise its voice. It lowers the water.
