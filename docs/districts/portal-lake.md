# Portal Lake

## The Heart of Ayerstone

Portal Lake is the vast central lake of Ayerstone, roughly two miles across. It dominates the center of the city and shapes nearly every district around it.

Above the lake hangs the Waterplane Portal: an immense glowing blue-white vortex connected to the Plane of Elemental Water. The portal floats 50 to 100 feet in the air near Noble Row, roughly a hundred feet from the southern ledge of the lake basin.

Water pours endlessly from the portal as a massive waterfall into the lake below.

## Civic Importance

Portal Lake feeds the canals, reservoirs, fountains, waterhouses, public cisterns, river channels, and great waterfalls of Ayerstone. Without it, the city would not function.

## Appearance

The lake is surrounded by noble terraces, waterworks, bridges, gardens, temples, and overlooks. The roar of falling water can be heard across Noble Row and parts of Crownward.

At night the portal glows like a blue-white star over the water.

## Public Beliefs

Many citizens view Portal Lake as a civic miracle. Sailors treat it as a sign that Ayerstone is favored. Engineers treat it as a responsibility. Priests argue about what it means.

## Local Saying

> Stone builds a city. Water keeps it alive.
