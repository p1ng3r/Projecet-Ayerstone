# Driftwall

## The City Beneath the City

*Most people come to Ayerstone looking for something. Most of them arrive here first.*

Driftwall occupies much of the southeastern edge of Ayerstone's central island. It is pressed against retaining walls, cliff edges, old foundations, forgotten tunnels, industrial spillover, and the rougher routes between the docks, markets, and lower wards.

Originally called **the Drift** because it collected sailors, immigrants, runaways, refugees, workers, adventurers, dreamers, failures, and people who did not fit anywhere else, the district became **Driftwall** as homes and shops were built against the great walls supporting the upper city.

## Appearance

Buildings are packed tightly together and built from wood, salvaged stone, ship timber, reused hull planks, canvas, old doors, scrap metal, and whatever else can be made to hold.

Bridges connect rooftops. Laundry hangs between buildings. Colorful banners stretch over narrow streets. Small shrines sit in alley corners. Stairways double back on themselves. Some streets are barely wide enough for two people to pass.

To outsiders, Driftwall looks chaotic.

To locals, it follows generations of hard-earned logic.

## Neighborhoods

### Lower Drift

The poorest section, closest to the edge and lowest retaining walls. Homes are patched together, streets are narrow, and people know exactly which planks not to step on.

### Lantern Cuts

A web of alleyways lit by hanging lanterns, food stalls, repair shops, cheap rooms, fortune tellers, and questionable doorways.

### Wallside

Homes and shops built directly into or against the old retaining walls. Some cellars open into tunnels no map admits exist.

### Smokefall Edge

The edge closest to the industrial districts, where soot, forge dust, and cheap rent mix together.

## The Gobble Inn

The Gobble Inn is the most beloved landmark in Driftwall.

It is owned and run by **Bilk Cheffy**, a goblin comfort-food master. Before opening the inn fifteen years ago, Bilk cooked for royalty, nobles, merchants, and dignitaries. Then he left that life behind.

Many first-time visitors walk in expecting the worst food in Ayerstone and leave stunned. Everything is cooked fresh daily because Bilk refuses to do it any other way.

The inn is warm, crowded, loud, and smells like stews, roasted root vegetables, fresh bread, spiced meats, and sweet sauces.

**Kaelyra Vosswick** is the head waitress, accountant, and practical manager who keeps the Gobble Inn running. Bilk may be the heart of the inn, but Kaelyra is the spine.

## The Shadow Brotherhood

Driftwall is watched by the **Shadow Brotherhood**, a notorious criminal organization that grew from the old Elven Brotherhood.

Once, the guild had a more elegant reputation. That changed when its old power structure collapsed.

Important names:

- **Vaelis Moonshadow** - old elven guildmaster from the Brotherhood's earlier age.
- **Corvin Veyl** - human guild boss, now dead.
- **Sylas Thornveil** - current hidden master, often called the Rat King.

Sylas is rumored to be a wererat. Many whisper that he has converted some of his closest followers with the same curse.

Since Sylas took power, many citizens see the Brotherhood as closer to assassins than thieves, though this is only partly true. The guild still handles smuggling, theft, information, black-market goods, disappearances, and quiet violence.

## Tunnels

Rumors say Sylas discovered an old tunnel network beneath Driftwall and now uses it to move throughout Ayerstone unseen.

The tunnels may connect to:

- Old foundations
- Drainage routes
- Forgotten cellars
- Deepstone passages
- Smuggler holes
- Collapsed service roads
- Places older than Ayerstone's current streets

No honest map includes them.

## Law and Survival

The Irystone Watch patrols Driftwall, but it does not control it. Locals often rely on neighbors, family networks, tavern owners, block elders, and quiet arrangements.

A stranger asking too many questions may be ignored, watched, helped, robbed, or all four in that order.

## Culture

Driftwall survives through mutual obligation. A person who eats today may feed someone else tomorrow. Neighbors share roofs, tools, rumors, childcare, warnings, and grudges.

The district has more music than money and more pride than comfort.

## Adventure Hooks

- A tunnel entrance opens beneath a bakery after a small quake.
- Kaelyra asks for help after someone tries to buy the Gobble Inn's accounts.
- A Shadow Brotherhood courier dies with a noble seal hidden in his boot.
- Bilk refuses to cook for a visiting noble and the refusal becomes political.
- A Driftwall child knows a secret route into Crownward.
- Someone is turning old Elven Brotherhood symbols into new warnings.
- A shipment stolen from the Grand Docks reappears in a Driftwall shrine.

## Local Sayings

> You arrive through the Docks. You stay because of Driftwall.

> Driftwall remembers who shared bread.

> If the street goes nowhere, you are almost there.
