## Overview

House Starweaver is the great house of routes, horizons, exploration, shipping, and Arkflight commerce. Its wealth rests on ships, maps, berths, cargo contracts, long-distance trade, route intelligence, and the immense influence of the **Celestial Trade and Shipping Consortium**.

Where other houses control stone, law, water, metal, or soldiers, Starweaver controls possibility. A new route, a reliable port, a safe current, or a hidden shortcut can make a fortune. House Starweaver has spent generations turning horizons into ledgers.

The house is most visible in Noble Row, the Grand Docks, and the Upper Docks, where its marble trade halls, star-glass balconies, blue lanterns, route offices, and ship banners are part of daily life in Ayerstone.

## Public Identity

Starweaver buildings are designed around views. Their estates include observatory balconies, glass-roofed map rooms, open courtyards, wind terraces, star chambers, hanging gardens, and route galleries.

Their spaces always seem ready to depart. Doors open onto balconies. Balconies open onto sky. Sky opens onto the Void.

The house colors are **midnight blue**, **silver**, and **white-gold**. Their preferred symbols are stars, open horizons, lanterns, compasses, silver-thread routes, and ships passing beyond a curved line.

## Heraldry

The Starweaver crest is a silver star above a white-gold horizon line, crossed by a compass needle and surrounded by three small route-marks.

Some captains use a simpler field sign: a blue lantern beneath a silver star.

## Motto

> Every horizon opens.

Older house saying:

> The route exists. You simply have not found it.

## Lady Celestia Starweaver

### Matriarch of House Starweaver

Lady Celestia Starweaver is the public face of House Starweaver and one of the most powerful figures in Ayerstone.

She is known by many titles:

- Matriarch of House Starweaver
- Keeper of Horizons
- Warden of Ayerstone Routes
- Mistress of the Open Horizon Fleet
- Head of the Celestial Trade and Shipping Consortium

Celestia is graceful, controlled, and difficult to read. She speaks softly enough that others lean in to listen. She rarely wastes movement. She rarely repeats herself. In public she presents as a refined half-elven noblewoman of immense wealth, influence, and composure.

Merchants respect her. Captains fear disappointing her. Rivals call her cold. Sailors who have survived her contracts call her fair, but never forgiving.

## Public Reputation

To ordinary citizens, Lady Celestia is the woman whose ships keep goods moving through the Void.

To merchants, she is opportunity and danger in the same silk glove.

To captains, she is the reason a poor crew can become rich and the reason a foolish crew can lose everything.

To the Crown, she is indispensable.

## Celestial Trade and Shipping Consortium

The **Celestial Trade and Shipping Consortium** is the largest and most visible Starweaver institution in Ayerstone. Its headquarters dominate the Upper Grand Docks: a massive white-marble trade hall where merchants rent booths, captains negotiate contracts, clerks register cargo, route officials review schedules, and wealthy buyers avoid the crush of Merchant's Reach.

The Consortium handles:

- Shipping contracts
- Cargo registry
- Route licensing
- Merchant booth rentals
- High-value cargo security
- Dockside arbitration
- Elite buyer access
- Convoy contracts
- Travel documentation
- Route intelligence

It is not the only trade power in Ayerstone, but it is the one everyone has to account for.

## The Open Horizon Fleet

House Starweaver maintains the **Open Horizon Fleet**, a collection of merchant vessels, charter ships, exploration craft, courier ships, convoy leaders, survey vessels, and private security escorts.

The fleet is not a navy in the royal sense, but it is powerful enough that pirates, rival merchants, and small city-states take it seriously.

Its captains are expected to be profitable, discreet, and brave in that order.

## Holdings

House Starweaver influence includes:

- Noble Row estates overlooking Portal Lake
- The Celestial Trade and Shipping Consortium hall on the Upper Docks
- Route offices throughout the Grand Docks
- Blue-lantern warehouses
- Private berths
- Star chart archives
- Merchant booth contracts
- Courier houses
- Ship shares
- Exploration charters
- Trade missions across the Chainbound Isles

## Starweaver Estates

The main Starweaver estate in Noble Row is built to face both Portal Lake and the open Void. Its upper balconies are used for star readings, private negotiations, and ceremonial departures. Its interior contains map galleries, route rooms, glass floors showing water below, and chambers where old compasses hang like relics.

A guest is never invited into a Starweaver map room by accident.

## Fashion

Starweaver fashion favors long coats, layered fabrics, silver embroidery, compass motifs, star patterns, flowing scarves, hidden map pockets, fine boots, and jewelry shaped like navigation tools.

Formal Starweaver dress often includes star-glass brooches and silver-thread route embroidery that traces actual trade paths.

Captains in house service often carry blue lantern charms, not as decoration, but as proof of contract.

## Traditions

### First Map

Starweaver children create their first map before adulthood. It may be a room, garden, street, dock, island, or imaginary route, but it must show how to get somewhere worth reaching.

### Blue Lantern Vow

Captains in Starweaver service keep blue lanterns lit from old ceremonial flames. A captain who lets the lantern go dark during a voyage is considered either unlucky or careless.

### The One Free Voyage

Guests at Starweaver tables are sometimes asked:

> If given one free voyage, where would you go?

The answer is remembered.

## Allies and Rivals

### Common Allies

- **House Aurelian**, through royal trade necessity and city logistics.
- **House Blackwake**, through convoy escort and anti-piracy contracts.
- **House Stormglass**, through ship design, repair, and experimental Arkflight craft.
- **House Veyr**, through financing, insurance, and cargo bonds.

### Common Rivals

- **Independent captains**, when they resist Consortium fees.
- **Merchant Reach cartels**, when Starweaver trade bypasses the market.
- **House Tidereach**, when water routes and shipping tariffs overlap.
- **House Ironmantle**, when ore transport contracts become too expensive.

## Famous Dock Tale

One of the most repeated stories in the Grand Docks concerns a gnome accountant caught embezzling from Celestial Trade and Shipping.

According to dockside legend, Lady Celestia herself brought the culprit before the merchants, sailors, travelers, and captains gathered at the center of the docks. What happened next changes with every telling, but all versions agree on one thing: the gnome was never seen in Starweaver service again.

Some insist the story is exaggerated.

No one embezzles from Celestial Trade twice.

## Current Interests

House Starweaver is especially interested in:

- Vael'Tharis expeditions
- Chainbound Isle routes
- Glacivane research transport
- Dawnmarrow rumors
- Arkengine instability near strange magical zones
- New markets outside established routes
- Keeping the Grand Docks profitable and orderly

## Adventure Hooks

- A Starweaver courier ship arrives with its crew missing and cargo sealed from the inside.
- Lady Celestia quietly hires adventurers to recover a lost route ledger before rivals find it.
- A blue lantern from a vanished captain relights itself in a Starweaver warehouse.
- A merchant booth at the Consortium sells goods from a port that no longer exists.
- A Starweaver map changes when placed near an Ark Compass.
- Independent captains accuse the Consortium of hiding a safe route to a dangerous island.
- A contract signed under Starweaver seal names the party as escort for cargo no one is allowed to open.
