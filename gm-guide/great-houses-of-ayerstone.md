# The Great Houses of Ayerstone

*Power, Bloodlines, Trade, and Secrets Among the Stars*

Ayerstone is ruled by a crown, but no one in the city is foolish enough to believe the crown rules alone.

The throne belongs to **House Aurelian**, an ancient and respected bloodline whose kings have watched over the floating world-shard for generations. The laws name the king as sovereign. The palace names him protector. The people bow when he passes. Yet in the marble halls of Crownward, in the counting rooms of Merchant’s Reach, in the dock offices above the Grand Docks, and in the locked vaults beneath old noble estates, another truth is spoken in quieter voices.

Ayerstone is held together by the Great Houses.

There are nine of them. Some began as merchants, adventurers, shipwrights, bankers, industrialists, or privateers who became too wealthy to ignore. Only **House Aurelian** and **House Marruk** possess true blood claims old enough to matter in noble law, but wealth has a way of rewriting history. A profitable house with ships, contracts, soldiers, water rights, and secrets can become noble soon enough if everyone else is afraid to say otherwise.

Officially, the Great Houses do not maintain private armies. Officially, they do not keep spies, warships, assassins, informants, enforcers, smugglers, or hidden agents. Officially, their guards are bodyguards, their fleets are trade escorts, their blades are household retainers, and their informants are loyal servants.

No one believes this.

The houses meet through the ancient political body known as **The Table of Chains**, where each Great House speaks with one sealed voice before the crown. The name is older than most surviving charters. Some say it honors the adamantite chains that bind Ayerstone’s outer islands to the world-shard. Others say it refers to the debts, oaths, bloodlines, contracts, and secrets that bind the houses to one another.

Both meanings are true.

If the houses are asked who truly rules Ayerstone, they will smile politely and say that power rests with those who keep the city fed, defended, financed, supplied, and connected to the void. If the king is asked the same question, he will say that all power flows from the throne.

Both answers are true.

Both answers are dangerous.

At the center of this delicate balance stands **King Lyrandel Utlar Azoun VII** of House Aurelian, a distant monarch with an ancient crown, a disputed future, and a secret written in infernal law. His birth should have been impossible: a tiefling child born to clearly human and elven parents. Hidden in a royal vault is the explanation — an old contract dating back to **King Azoun II**, who made a terrible bargain connected to the opening of the Water Portal. For eleven centuries, the debt slept.

Now it has awakened in the blood of the king.

Guarding that truth is **Olstar Vin-Har**, a powerful mage of House Aurelian and keeper of the vault where the infernal contract is sealed. For years he has studied infernal law, sending adventurers deep into the void in search of forgotten tomes, dead civilizations, devil-script tablets, and loopholes old enough to challenge Hell itself.

Yet the crown’s secret is not the only one capable of breaking Ayerstone.

Among the Great Houses, none is richer than **House Starweaver**. Publicly, they are merely another noble trading company with shipping contracts, portal access, and elegant manners. Privately, many believe they are the strongest power in the city. There are whispers that **Lady Celestia Starweaver** could crush any three houses on a bad day, and all of them on a good one.

Those whispers are closer to the truth than anyone knows.

For Lady Celestia is no mere half-elven merchant matriarch. She is something older, greater, and far more dangerous: an ancient celestial dragon hidden behind silk, contracts, and a patient smile.

These are the Great Houses of Ayerstone: nine powers bound by law, rivalry, debt, blood, trade, fear, and secrets.

Together, they keep the city alive.

Together, they may tear it apart.

## House Aurelian

*The Royal House of Ayerstone*

**Motto:** *The Stone Endures*  
**Colors:** White marble, royal purple, polished gold, amethyst violet  
**Symbol:** A golden crown above a faceted floating stone marked with the scales and sword of justice

House Aurelian is the royal house of Ayerstone and the oldest surviving dynasty on the world-shard. Many claim the Aurelians were among the first settlers to stand upon Ayerstone’s broken stone, long before its ports became crowded with voidships, long before merchant princes bought their way into nobility, and long before the Great Houses learned to smile at one another across the Table of Chains.

Unlike most of the Great Houses, House Aurelian has a true blood claim. Its right to rule is not founded on wealth, contracts, shipyards, privateers, food, or trade routes, but on history. The Aurelians present themselves as the lawful continuation of Ayerstone itself: old, distant, dignified, and difficult to move.

Their public face is aristocratic restraint. House Aurelian rarely pushes its power openly. It does not need to. The crown still commands ancient respect, and even those who plot against the king are careful to bow before him in public.

The current monarch is **King Lyrandel Utlar Azoun VII**, thirty-one years of age, unmarried, and far younger than the burden placed upon him. He was once engaged, but when war broke out with the **Grelkin Empire**, his intended bride was murdered, leaving the king with a grief he has never fully set aside.

The public knows the king is a tiefling. That fact alone has shaken the royal image of House Aurelian. His father was human. His mother was elven. No ordinary bloodline should have produced the horns, infernal marks, and strange inheritance now visible in Azoun VII. The court speaks politely of destiny, mystery, and old magic. The common people are less careful.

The truth is worse.

Deep beneath the grand Aurelian compound lies a hidden vault known only to the king and **Olstar Vin-Har**, a powerful royal mage. Within that vault rests a contract written on strange leathery skin, preserved by wards older than most living houses. The document records the bargain made by **King Azoun II** when the Water Portal was opened. Azoun II knowingly made the bargain, but the contract was written with infernal cleverness, full of traps, omissions, and meanings that only became dangerous long after the ink had dried.

The known half of the bargain gave Ayerstone the Water Portal.

The other half is missing.

No one knows exactly what the demons were promised. No one knows whether the birth of Azoun VII is the first true manifestation of the bargain, or merely the most visible. Some whisper that the real sign has always been the horror that emerges from the Water Portal every few years: a vast kraken-like tentacle that pushes through the portal and claims a citizen before vanishing back into impossible depths.

Olstar Vin-Har has spent years studying infernal law in secret. Though he is a mage, many say he has the soul of a paladin, sworn in all but title to the king and to the god of justice. He sends adventurers deep into the void to recover ancient tomes, devil-script tablets, dead empire records, and forgotten legal codes that might explain how to break, challenge, or survive the contract.

His fear is simple.

If the contract is not broken, Ayerstone itself may one day belong to demons.

Politically, House Aurelian stands in a dangerous position. **House Marruk** controls much of the royal security, serving officially as the king’s military arm. The king mostly trusts them, but House Aurelian resents how much power Marruk holds inside the palace itself. No house is more hated by Aurelian than Marruk, though neither side can afford open conflict.

For practical support, Aurelian often relies on **House Emberhall**, whose respected industrial strength gives the crown weapons, construction, machinery, and leverage outside Marruk’s reach.

House Aurelian remains ancient. It remains respected. It remains royal.

But beneath its marble halls, the stone remembers a bargain.

And the stone may not endure forever.

### Notable House Aurelian NPCs

#### Lord Caedran Aurelian

*The Marble Hawk*

**Ancestry:** Human  
**Role:** Royal cousin, court duelist, quiet political operator  
**Age:** 36  
**Public Reputation:** Perfect noble gentleman, war veteran, defender of royal dignity  
**Private Truth:** He is building a secret loyalist network to free the crown from House Marruk’s grip.

Lord Caedran Aurelian is everything the public expects an Aurelian nobleman to be: composed, elegant, polite, beautifully dressed, and almost impossible to read. He speaks softly, never raises his voice, and treats even his enemies with immaculate courtesy. This makes people underestimate him.

They should not.

Caedran is one of the finest duelists in Crownward, trained with rapier, cane-sword, dagger, and pistol. During the war with the Grelkin Empire, he served as a royal battlefield envoy, carrying orders through broken siege lines and negotiating prisoner exchanges under white banners. He survived three assassination attempts and killed the last assassin with a dinner knife while still wearing formal court gloves.

At court, Caedran is known as **the Marble Hawk** because he watches everything from stillness. He rarely speaks during meetings of the Table of Chains, but when he does, his words are sharp enough to change votes. Many nobles consider him old-fashioned, loyal, and harmlessly ceremonial.

That is exactly what he wants them to think.

Caedran believes House Marruk has become too powerful inside the palace. He knows the king mostly trusts them, but Caedran sees every Marruk guard in the royal compound as a chain around House Aurelian’s throat. To counter them, he has begun quietly building the **White Oath**, a hidden network of loyal servants, retired guards, minor nobles, old war veterans, and royal sympathizers who answer to the crown before any Great House.

He is not plotting against King Azoun VII. He is plotting for him.

Caedran knows the public shame around the king’s tiefling bloodline has weakened the throne. Unlike many Aurelian nobles, he does not resent the king’s infernal features. He sees Azoun VII as a wounded but rightful monarch who must be protected until the crown can stand fully on its own again.

Caedran does not know the full truth of the infernal contract, but he suspects there is a hidden reason behind the king’s birth. He has noticed Olstar Vin-Har’s secret expeditions, missing royal funds, and coded requests for obscure legal tomes. Caedran has not confronted him yet.

He is waiting until he has enough leverage to force the truth.

**Appearance:** Caedran is tall, lean, and severe, with pale skin, dark silver-streaked hair, and sharp gray eyes. He wears white-and-purple Aurelian court clothing with gold trim, but always carries a slim sword-cane capped with an amethyst stone.

**Personality:** Calm, observant, controlled, loyal, and ruthless when necessary. He never threatens people directly. He simply makes them understand what will happen if they continue.

**Table Use:** Caedran works well as a royal patron for discreet missions, a dueling rival, a court ally who tests the party before trusting them, a secret backer against House Marruk, or a noble who may discover too much about the infernal contract.

**Quote:** “Power is not always held by the hand gripping the sword. Sometimes it belongs to the one who knows when the sword must remain sheathed.”

#### Lady Vaelessa Aurelian

*The Amethyst Thorn*

**Ancestry:** Half-Elf  
**Role:** Royal archivist, curse-scholar, keeper of forbidden genealogies  
**Age:** 42  
**Public Reputation:** Elegant historian and guardian of royal records  
**Private Truth:** She has found evidence that Azoun VII may not be the last Aurelian touched by the old bargain.

Lady Vaelessa Aurelian is the master of House Aurelian’s public archives, bloodline records, marriage contracts, old royal decrees, succession histories, and forbidden family documents. To outsiders, she appears to be a refined noble scholar with a dry wit and a taste for old books.

Inside House Aurelian, she is feared.

Vaelessa has an almost supernatural memory for names, dates, births, deaths, inheritances, debts, and scandals. She can destroy a noble with a sentence by proving their grandfather’s marriage was never legally witnessed, their claim to land was improperly transferred, or their bloodline title rests on a forged charter.

She never needs to lie.

The truth is usually cruel enough.

Vaelessa was among the first members of House Aurelian to understand that King Azoun VII’s tiefling birth was not random. She began secretly studying old royal genealogies and discovered gaps: missing children, erased stillbirth records, sealed medical notes, strange references to “stone-fever,” “horn-sign,” and “water dreams” scattered across centuries of Aurelian history.

She does not know the full contents of the infernal contract, but she has reached a terrifying conclusion.

Azoun VII may not be the first manifestation of the bargain.

He may simply be the first one House Aurelian failed to hide.

Vaelessa is outwardly loyal to the king, but her loyalty is colder than Caedran’s. She believes House Aurelian must survive even if individual Aurelians must be sacrificed. If the king can be saved, she will help save him. If he becomes the legal doorway through which demons can claim Ayerstone, she will look for a way to remove him without destroying the crown.

This makes her useful, brilliant, and dangerous.

Vaelessa has begun collecting evidence from old midwives’ journals, sealed chapel records, water-damaged birth ledgers, and forbidden family portraits. Some of these records suggest that certain Aurelian children were born with strange eyes, vestigial horns, infernal shadows, or dreams of drowning beneath an endless black sea.

She keeps her most damning findings in a hidden archive called **the Thorn Ledger**.

If Olstar Vin-Har is trying to break the contract through infernal law, Vaelessa is trying to understand it through blood.

**Appearance:** Vaelessa is graceful and sharp-featured, with long black hair threaded with silver, pale violet eyes, and a calm expression that rarely changes. She wears layered white, purple, and gold gowns with high collars, amethyst jewelry, and finger-rings shaped like tiny thorns.

**Personality:** Elegant, precise, terrifyingly intelligent, patient, and emotionally restrained. She is not cruel for pleasure, but she believes sentiment is dangerous when a kingdom is at stake.

**Table Use:** Vaelessa works well as a source of hidden royal history, a dangerous quest-giver, a political interrogator, a rival to Olstar Vin-Har, a noble who may help or betray the king depending on what the party discovers, or the keeper of clues about older Aurelian manifestations.

**Quote:** “Blood remembers what history edits out.”
